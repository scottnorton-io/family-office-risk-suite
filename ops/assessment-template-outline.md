# Assessment Template Outline – Family Office Cyber & Privacy Posture

> This is the outline structure for the primary assessment deliverable. It
> should be board-ready, concise, and clear.

---

## 1. Executive Summary (2–3 pages)

- Context and objectives.
- High-level risk posture (visual heatmap).
- Top 5–10 prioritized actions.
- Key themes (e.g., “people and process,” “vendors,” “home tech”).

---

## 2. Scope & Approach

- What was in scope:
  - Locations (office, home office, other).
  - People (roles, not names unless agreed).
  - Systems and platforms.
  - Vendors.
- Methods:
  - Interviews (who and how many).
  - Documentation reviewed.
  - Light-touch technical checks (as applicable).
- Timeframe of the assessment.

---

## 3. Environment Overview

- High-level description of the family office:
  - Size, structure, key functions.
- Key assets:
  - Data types (financial, personal, legal, etc.).
  - Critical systems and platforms.
- High-level architecture:
  - Where data lives.
  - Where key risks cluster.

---

## 4. Risk Posture Summary

- Visual heatmap (by domain or category).
- Short narrative describing:
  - Strengths.
  - Gaps.
  - Emerging concerns.

Possible domains:

- People & behavior
- Devices & endpoints
- Accounts & identity
- Home and travel tech
- Vendors & platforms
- Governance & process

---

## 5. Detailed Findings & Recommendations

For each domain:

- **Finding** – short statement of what is observed.
- **Why it matters** – consequences in plain language.
- **Current state** – how things are done today.
- **Recommended action** – specific, practical change.
- **Effort / impact rating** – to help prioritize.

---

## 6. Vendor & Platform Overview (if in scope)

- List of critical vendors and platforms.
- Simple risk rating (R/Y/G) for each.
- Key comments and any immediate concerns.
- Recommended follow-up (e.g., deeper review, renegotiation, additional controls).

---

## 7. Governance & Operating Model Observations

- Decision clarity:
  - Who decides what, where ambiguity exists.
- Process maturity:
  - Where things work well.
  - Where work is ad hoc or fragile.
- Documentation:
  - What exists, what doesn’t, and why it matters.

---

## 8. Prioritized Action Plan (90-Day Focus)

- Table of actions:
  - Action.
  - Owner (role).
  - Suggested timeframe.
  - Notes / dependencies.

Highlight:

- “No-regrets” actions – low effort, high impact.
- Actions that unlock broader improvements.

---

## 9. Longer-Term Considerations (6–24 months)

- Areas that require more structural or strategic change.
- Recommended pacing so that change is sustainable.

---

## 10. Appendices

- Interview and meeting list.
- Documents reviewed.
- Additional detail for technical readers (if appropriate).
