# Cross-Border Residency & Tax Exposure Matrix  
**Version 1.0 — Complete Document**

---

## 1. Purpose  
Families with multinational lives face complex tax, legal, and residency risks.  
This matrix provides a structured view of exposures by jurisdiction, activity, and entity.

---

## 2. Core Residency Triggers  
Common residency triggers across jurisdictions include:

- Physical presence  
- Domicile  
- Center of vital interests  
- Permanent home availability  
- Visa/tax status  
- Corporate directorships  
- Family ties  
- Asset ownership  

---

## 3. Exposure Matrix Example  

| Jurisdiction | Residency Trigger | Tax Exposure | Mitigation |
|--------------|-------------------|--------------|------------|
| U.S. | Substantial Presence Test | Global income | Monitor days; tax counsel |
| U.K. | Statutory Residence Test | Domicile exposure | Day tracking; domicile planning |
| UAE | Visa residence | Zero income tax | Maintain local presence |
| Switzerland | Cantonal rules | Lump-sum tax | Pre-agreements |

---

## 4. Multi-Jurisdictional Pitfalls  
- Accidental dual residency  
- Conflicting tax treaties  
- Corporate director risks  
- Trusts taxed as domiciled entities  
- Wealth reporting requirements  
- CRS/FATCA mismatches  

---

## 5. Monitoring Framework  
- Centralized travel log  
- Residency “traffic light” dashboard  
- Annual cross-border legal review  
- Coordination between tax counsel in all jurisdictions
