# Family Travel Security Playbook  
*For HNWI, Principals, Family Members, Staff, SFO, and MFO Environments*  
**Version 1.0 — Complete Document**

---

## 1. Purpose

High-net-worth individuals and family offices face elevated risks while traveling—especially internationally. Criminal groups, hostile intelligence actors, digital adversaries, kidnapping rings, and opportunistic scammers often target affluent travelers.

This playbook establishes a comprehensive framework for:

- Pre-travel planning  
- Digital and physical safety  
- Secure communications  
- Emergency response  
- Executive protection alignment  
- Post-travel sanitization  

It is written for principals, spouses, children, staff, and security teams.

---

## 2. Threat Landscape for HNWI Travelers

### 2.1 Common Threats
- SIM-swapping  
- Device theft or covert access  
- Shoulder surfing  
- Airport Wi-Fi interception  
- Fake charging stations (“juice jacking”)  
- Border-device inspection  
- “Express kidnapping”  
- Hotel room covert entry  
- Criminal surveillance on arrival  
- Deepfake voice fraud using travel photos/audio  
- Targeted social engineering while abroad  

### 2.2 High-Risk Geographies
Classifications (Low / Medium / High / Extreme) based on:

- Political stability  
- Corruption  
- Digital surveillance  
- Kidnapping statistics  
- Hostility to Western travelers  
- Availability of emergency services  
- Airport safety and reliability

## 3. Pre-Travel Requirements

### 3.1 Travel Security Briefing
Mandatory for all family members and staff:

- Local risks  
- Common scams  
- Safe transport  
- Digital surveillance expectations  
- Embassy/consulate locations  
- Local emergency numbers  
- Border search rules  

### 3.2 Digital Preparation Checklist
- Backup all devices  
- Update iOS/Android/macOS before travel  
- Remove sensitive documents  
- Remove financial apps if possible  
- Create separate “Travel Apple ID / Google Account”  
- Enable travel-specific MFA tokens  
- Set up “burner” or temp devices for high-risk travel  
- Disable biometric unlock if traveling to hostile jurisdictions  

### 3.3 Home Risk Reduction Before Departure
- Remove travel posts from social media  
- Delay posting travel photos until return  
- Ensure home security system is active  
- Adjust household staff schedules  
- Run a perimeter check with physical security team

## 4. Devices for Travel

### 4.1 Recommended Device Strategy
| Destination Risk Level | Phone | Laptop | Watch | Notes |
|------------------------|--------|--------|--------|-------|
| Low | Personal | Personal | Optional | Standard precautions |
| Medium | Personal | Travel laptop | Optional | Limit data exposure |
| High | Travel phone | Travel laptop | No | Remove sensitive apps |
| Extreme | Burner phone | Secure laptop | No | Assume monitoring |

### 4.2 “Travel Device” Requirements
- No financial data  
- No sensitive messages  
- No family documents  
- Limited contact list  
- Strong passcode (minimum 8 digits)  
- VPN with kill switch  
- Encrypted storage only  
- No biometrics  

---

## 5. Airport & Transit Security

### 5.1 At Airports
- Avoid public Wi-Fi unless behind secure VPN  
- Use only personal charging cables  
- Never leave bags unattended  
- Prefer lounge areas over terminal seating  
- Avoid speaking about plans or assets in public  

### 5.2 In Transit (Plane/Train)
- Do NOT type passwords in confined proximity  
- Do NOT review investment reports or sensitive communications  
- Cover laptop screen with privacy filter  
- Enable Airplane Mode + disable Wi-Fi during taxi/takeoff  

---

## 6. Hotel & Accommodation Security

### 6.1 Room Selection
- Avoid ground floor  
- Avoid floors 2–4 (common break-in targets)  
- Avoid rooms near stairwells  
- Prefer mid-building rooms  

### 6.2 In-Room Security Checklist
- Use hotel safe ONLY for non-critical items  
- Use personal travel safe for passports  
- Bring portable door jam for extra security  
- Cover peepholes  
- Disable smart TVs and voice assistant devices  
- Disconnect unknown Bluetooth devices  

### 6.3 Housekeeping Protocols
- Use “Do Not Disturb” unless required  
- Instruct hotel NOT to allow staff entry without principal present  
- Keep valuables locked and out of sight  

---

## 7. Digital Security During Travel

### 7.1 Connectivity
- Use mobile hotspot instead of hotel Wi-Fi  
- VPN always on  
- No banking or sensitive transactions abroad  
- Avoid logging into new or unfamiliar services  

### 7.2 Device Safety
- Use strong passcodes  
- Keep devices physically secured  
- Beware of shoulder-surfing at airports  
- Avoid discussing financial matters on calls  

### 7.3 Phishing & Fraud
- Expect targeted messages aligned with travel itinerary  
- Never click links for “hotel confirmations” sent unexpectedly  
- Confirm reservations only via original booking apps  

---

## 8. Transport Safety

### 8.1 Car Services
- Use trusted, vetted drivers  
- No ride-sharing in high-risk countries  
- Avoid “airport taxi line” in unsafe regions  
- Do not share itinerary with drivers  

### 8.2 Walking & Public Spaces
- Avoid walking alone at night  
- Avoid visible luxury items  
- Be aware of surveillance and tailing tactics  

---

## 9. Emergency Procedures

### For: theft, harassment, lost passport, medical emergency, kidnapping attempts

1. Move to a secure location  
2. Contact security team  
3. Contact embassy/consulate  
4. Lock down accounts  
5. Wipe devices remotely if stolen  
6. Document sequence of events  
7. Avoid social media posts  

---

## 10. Post-Travel Procedures

### 10.1 Device Sanitization
- Change all passwords  
- Review device logs  
- Remove travel accounts  
- Reset network settings  
- Run malware scan  
- Restore device from secure backup  

### 10.2 Exposure Assessment
- Review new vulnerabilities found  
- Document suspicious activity  
- Update digital footprint monitoring
