## 1. Purpose  
This Charter establishes the investment governance structure for HNWI, SFO, and MFO clients.  
Its goal is to ensure disciplined decision-making, risk-adjusted performance, and alignment between Principals, advisors, and the Family Office.

This Charter defines:
- Roles and responsibilities  
- Decision rights  
- Performance oversight  
- Reporting frameworks  
- Investment principles  
- Risk appetite boundaries  

---

## 2. Investment Philosophy  

### Core Principles:
1. **Preservation-first**  
   Protect capital before seeking returns.

2. **Strategic, not tactical**  
   No short-term speculation or reactive behavior.

3. **Evidence-based**  
   Decisions follow quantitative and qualitative analysis.

4. **Alignment with family mission**  
   Investments must support long-term values and lifestyle.

5. **Risk transparency**  
   All downside scenarios must be understood before investing.

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities  

### **Principal**
- Defines long-term financial objectives  
- Approves major investment decisions  
- Sets risk appetite  

### **Chief Investment Officer / Investment Lead**
- Oversees investment strategy  
- Conducts manager selection and monitoring  
- Manages rebalancing  
- Leads due diligence  
- Provides reporting  

### **Family Office Director**
- Ensures governance compliance  
- Coordinates across advisors  
- Provides operational support  

### **External Advisors**
- Provide specialized expertise  
- Cannot execute trades without authorization  
- Must disclose conflicts  

---

## 4. Risk Boundaries  
| Risk Type | Limit |
|-----------|--------|
| Equity allocation | Max 60% unless Principal approves |
| Single investment | Max 5% NAV |
| Illiquid holdings | Max 25% NAV |
| Leverage | Not permitted unless explicitly approved |

---

## 5. Investment Committee (IC)  
Meets quarterly.  
Responsibilities:
- Review performance  
- Evaluate new opportunities  
- Assess risks  
- Approve major changes  
- Review liquidity position  
- Validate alignment with family objectives  

---

## 6. Documentation Requirements  
Each investment requires:
- Full due diligence memo  
- Risk scoring  
- Fee & liquidity analysis  
- Approval record  
- Ongoing monitoring plan
