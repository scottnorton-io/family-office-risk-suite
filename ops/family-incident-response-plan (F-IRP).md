# Family Incident Response Plan (F-IRP)
**Version 1.0 — Complete Document**

---

## 1. Purpose
This Family Incident Response Plan (F-IRP) defines the procedures, responsibilities, and escalation pathways for cybersecurity, privacy, operational, financial, and physical security incidents involving HNWI, Single-Family Offices (SFO), or Multi-Family Offices (MFO).  
Its purpose is to minimize damage, preserve safety, ensure continuity, and restore normal operations with minimal impact.

---

## 2. Scope
The F-IRP applies to:
- All family members  
- Personal assistants  
- Household staff  
- Advisors  
- IT and cybersecurity partners  
- Executive protection (EP) teams  
- Domestic and international residences  
- Travel operations  
- Digital assets, accounts, communications, and identities  

---

## 3. Incident Categories

### **3.1 Cybersecurity Incidents**
- Account compromise  
- SIM-swap  
- Device theft or infection  
- Smart home intrusion  
- Unauthorized access  

### **3.2 Privacy & Exposure Incidents**
- Doxxing  
- Leaked addresses  
- Leaked personal photos  
- Media exposure  
- Data broker rediscovery  

### **3.3 Financial Incidents**
- Fraudulent transactions  
- Wire-transfer tampering  
- Identity-theft banking events  

### **3.4 Physical & Safety Incidents**
- Harassment  
- Stalking  
- Residence breach  
- Suspicious persons  
- Travel security failures  

---

## 4. Incident Classification

| Level | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **L1 – Advisory** | Low severity | Phishing attempt caught pre-click |
| **L2 – Moderate** | Exposure but no damage | Credential leak in a breach dump |
| **L3 – Serious** | Real compromise | Email account access confirmed |
| **L4 – Critical** | Life/safety impacted | Stalker at residence |
| **L5 – Crisis** | Immediate danger | Kidnap attempt, violent threat |

---

## 5. Roles & Responsibilities

### **5.1 Family Office Command**
Responsible for decision-making, coordination, and authentication.

### **5.2 Cybersecurity Lead**
- Validates incidents  
- Performs forensic triage  
- Coordinates account/device lockdowns  

### **5.3 Executive Protection**
- Responds to physical threats  
- Coordinates safe locations  
- Handles travel emergencies  

### **5.4 Family Members**
- Immediately report incidents  
- Follow instructions without deviation  
- Maintain secure communications  

---

## 6. Response Workflow

### **Step 1 — Detect**
- Alerts from monitoring  
- Family/staff reporting  
- Vendor notifications  

### **Step 2 — Contain**
- Suspend compromised accounts  
- Freeze financial activity  
- Lock down devices  
- Secure physical perimeter  

### **Step 3 — Validate**
- Confirm scope  
- Identify affected assets  
- Determine the origin  
- Evaluate threat actor persistence  

### **Step 4 — Eradicate**
- Remove unauthorized access  
- Terminate malware or rogue apps  
- Reconfigure compromised systems  

### **Step 5 — Recover**
- Restore accounts  
- Re-enable operations  
- Monitor systems  
- Patch gaps  

### **Step 6 — Review**
- After-action report  
- Update controls  
- Update risk register  

---

## 7. Communications Channels

### **Secure (Primary)**
- Signal  
- iMessage  
- ProtonMail  
- 1Password secure notes  

### **Not Allowed**
- WhatsApp cloud backups  
- Email-based coordination  
- SMS  

---

## 8. Forensic Readiness
- Maintain device logs  
- Maintain backups  
- Keep breach documentation packet  
- Maintain vendor emergency contacts
