---

## 1. Purpose & Principles

- Purpose of the plan (what it is and is not).
- Principles:
  - Calm first, fast second.
  - Family priorities and privacy.
  - Coordination with existing advisors and vendors.

---

## 2. Scope

- Types of incidents covered:
  - Ransomware / device compromise.
  - Account takeover or wire fraud attempts.
  - Data leakage or loss of sensitive information.
  - Reputational incidents (public or social media-based).
- People, systems, and locations in scope.

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

- Internal roles:
  - Principal / family.
  - Family office leadership.
  - Key staff.
- External roles:
  - Legal counsel.
  - PR / communications.
  - Cyber insurance.
  - Incident response firm(s).
  - Key vendors (banks, custodians, platforms).

Clarify:

- Who leads the response.
- Who is informed and when.
- Who has authority to approve key actions.

---

## 4. First-Hour Response Playbooks

For each scenario type:

- **Checklist:**  
  - Immediate actions.
  - Actions to avoid (e.g., paying ransom without counsel).
- **Communication:**  
  - Who to notify and in what sequence.
  - Internal vs external messaging.
- **Containment:**  
  - Steps to limit spread or further damage.

---

## 5. First 24 Hours & Ongoing Response

- Stabilization steps.
- Coordination with vendors and advisors.
- Evidence preservation (for legal/forensic needs).
- External communication decisions (media, counterparties).

---

## 6. Decision Trees

Simple visual paths for:

- Whether to disconnect systems.
- Whether/when to engage external counsel or PR.
- Whether to notify external parties (regulators, law enforcement, etc., as applicable).

---

## 7. Tabletop Exercise Framework

- Scenario design.
- Roles to include.
- Expected timeline and format.
- Debrief structure:
  - What worked well.
  - What failed or was unclear.
  - Concrete improvements.

---

## 8. Improvement & Maintenance

- Cadence for:
  - Reviewing and updating the plan.
  - Testing critical elements (contact trees, escalation paths).
- Handling changes:
  - New vendors.
  - New locations or family members.
  - Major life or structural changes.

---

## 9. Appendices

- Contact lists (internal and external).
- Incident log template.
- Links or references to external obligations (if applicable).
