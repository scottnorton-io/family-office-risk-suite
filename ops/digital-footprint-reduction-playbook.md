## 1. Purpose

High-net-worth families face disproportionate digital exposure risks, including:

- Doxxing  
- Targeted scams  
- Harassment  
- Identity theft  
- Reputation damage  
- Physical safety threats  

This playbook provides a comprehensive lifecycle for discovering, reducing, monitoring, and maintaining a low digital footprint.

---

## 2. Digital Exposure Categories

### 2.1 Personal Identifiable Information (PII)
- Full name  
- Home address  
- Phone numbers  
- Date of birth  
- Email accounts  

### 2.2 Asset Exposure
- Home listings  
- Business relationships  
- Investment holdings  
- Public legal records  

### 2.3 Metadata Exposure
- Geolocation  
- Social graph  
- Behavioral patterns  

### 2.4 Breached Data
- Passwords  
- Email/password pairs  
- API tokens  

---

## 3. Discovery Phase

### 3.1 OSINT Sweep
Perform a structured OSINT investigation:

- Google/Bing search profiles  
- Reverse image searches  
- Data broker scans  
- Social media footprint  
- Username reuse patterns  
- Public records  
- Leaked credential databases  

Deliverable: **Digital Exposure Report**

---

## 4. Data Broker Removal Lifecycle

This lifecycle removes data from:

- Whitepages  
- Spokeo  
- MyLife  
- BeenVerified  
- PeopleFinder  
- Intelius  
- Radaris  
- FastPeopleSearch  
- TruthFinder  

### 4.1 Removal Steps
1. Identify broker  
2. Submit opt-out request  
3. Validate removal  
4. Document completion  
5. Add to monitored list  

### 4.2 Monitoring Cadence
- Monthly for the first 6 months  
- Quarterly after stabilization  

---

## 5. Social Media Minimization

### 5.1 Platform Checklist
- Lock down visibility  
- Change usernames  
- Remove location tags  
- Remove photos of home address  
- Remove family relationship data  
- Disable tagging  
- Enable two-factor authentication  

### 5.2 High-Risk Content Removal
- Location check-ins  
- Travel announcements  
- High-value asset photos  
- School/child information  

---

## 6. Email & Identity Hardening

### 6.1 Email Strategy
- Create separate identities for:  
  - Legal/financial  
  - Social media  
  - Shopping  
  - Travel  
- Use masked aliases where possible  
- MFA everywhere  

### 6.2 Password Strategy
- Password manager  
- Strong random passwords  
- Avoid password reuse  
- Rotate breached credentials immediately  

---

## 7. Phone Number & Messaging Security

### 7.1 Phone Numbers
- Use secondary numbers for public activity  
- Avoid linking primary number to social media  
- Remove phone number from public WHOIS records  

### 7.2 Messaging Hardening
- Signal or iMessage preferred  
- Disable SMS-based 2FA where possible  
- Avoid WhatsApp cloud backups  

---

## 8. Device & Location Safety

### 8.1 Mobile Devices
- Disable tracking  
- Limit app permissions  
- Avoid unknown Wi-Fi  
- Enable automatic updates  
- Disable ad ID tracking  

### 8.2 Home Address Protection
- Remove listing photos (via takedown requests)  
- Suppress property records when allowed  
- Use LLCs for real estate ownership when appropriate  

---

## 9. Search Engine Suppression

Where legal:

- Submit takedown requests  
- Request removal of outdated or harmful results  
- Request delisting under applicable privacy regulations  

---

## 10. Monitoring Strategy

### Cadence:
- Monthly the first quarter  
- Quarterly afterward  
- Immediately after major events (travel, media exposure)  

### Monitoring Includes:
- New data broker entries  
- New breach exposures  
- New OSINT findings  
- Changes in public records  
- Social media leaks  
- New impersonation attempts  

---

## 11. Emergency Scenarios

### 11.1 Doxxing Response
- Immediate takedown requests  
- Suppression of copied content  
- Social media escalation workflows  
- Notification to personal security if necessary  

### 11.2 Compromised Identity
- Freeze credit  
- Reset credentials  
- Notify banks  
- Update passwords  
- Enable high-risk monitoring  

---

- Remediation Checklist

- Social Media Audit  
- Device Configuration Baseline  
- Monthly Monitoring Summary
